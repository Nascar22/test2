When someone "LIKES" your page, it's their way of saying they are a fan and they are happy to tell their friends. . If someone only hits the "FOLLOW" button that person is saying they like your posts and want to receive more in their newsfeed, but they aren't wanting to publicly tell anyone they are following you. . ⚠️ Someone who "likes" your page will automatically be set to "follow" your page. ⚠️ They have the option to "unfollow" your page - this means they won't receive any of your content in their feed. ⚠️ Some people choose to click "FOLLOW" rather than "LIKE" - it keeps it private from everyone they know. . When your business is listed in places like Google or FB Groups, Facebook "LIKES" is what shows up in most places. . As long as you get the Algorithm right, Facebook "FOLLOWS" is how many people will see your posts. . What you definitely DON'T want is to have an awesome amount of "LIKES", and only have a few "FOLLOWS". If this happens, it means people have decided to unfollow you...which is really not what you want at all! .
