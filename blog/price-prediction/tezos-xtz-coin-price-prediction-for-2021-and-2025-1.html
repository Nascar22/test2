What is Tezos (XTZ)?

Arthur and Kathleen Breitman created the Tezos blockchain in 2014 through Dynamic Ledger Solutions, a startup they founded to develop Tezos.

Some say that XTZ is somewhat similar to Ethereum because both blockchain networks feature smart contracts. Still, there are differences. Tezos boasts a far more advanced infrastructure. In other words, it doesn’t require a hard fork or changing the network’s protocol that makes previously invalid blocks and transactions valid for any upgrade. Both Bitcoin and Ethereum can’t offer the same to their users. 

The Tezos team held an initial coin offering (ICO) in July 2017, raising the equivalent of $232 million (66,000 BTC and 361,000 ETH), the largest ICO. Following the ICO, the Tezos Foundation, based out of Switzerland, was created to launch the protocol.

The Tezos blockchain enables many features common to cryptocurrencies. Developers can use its software to run custom programming logic (smart contracts) and design new programs (decentralized applications) to replicate products and services. 

Tezos can be described as secure, upgradable, and built-to-last. XTZ is an open-source platform that is said to ensure outstanding accuracy due to its smart contracts. Moreover, its staking process involves the community – people participate in governing the network by “baking.” 

Main Features of Tezos (XTZ) includes;

  • Tezos is a self-amending decentralized platform for building dApps.
  • Network upgrade doesn’t require a hard fork, unlike other networks.
  • Tezos network confirmation is fast and cheap.
  • It can scale more transactions with smart contract enabled, unlike Ethereum.

Tezos (XTZ) Price Chart:

Making an XTZ price prediction may be difficult without assessing the token’s past performance. It helps to identify price fluctuation patterns and analyze which factors have the most significant influence. More so, Tezos news updates can affect the XTZ market movement and which will, in turn, affect the XTZ price. 

The Tezos coin started its path with a scandal. It was launched in 2017. The ICO took place in July and was among the largest in terms of the accumulated investments. The price was average, and it was bouncing between $1.7 and slightly more than $2. Moreover, the company had to deal with a lawsuit over ICO. There were several of these lawsuits. The blame was on XTZ founders for selling unregistered assets.

This, of course, affected the token price. It only had started to increase and reached the $5 benchmark in December 2017, when another complaint was filed. As a result, it dropped down to $3.9 in a couple of days.

However, the collapse wasn’t disastrous. Tezos price regained its position pretty fast and exceeded the maximum price at the beginning of January 2018, reaching $5.75. No matter how promising it may have been, 2018 happened to be a complicated year for all cryptocurrencies, with constant ups and downs. As a result, XTZ ended the year at a Tezos price USD of $0.5. 

What affected Tezos coin price significantly at the time was a set of regulations. Know Your Customer (KYC), and Anti-Money Laundering (AML) requirements were introduced in summer 2018. This became a problem for XTZ as no user data was collected during the ICO. 

In 2019 after a long bearish period, Tezos value finally started to regain its value. Three-fourth of the year (from March to December) were relatively prosperous. However, all cryptocurrencies were increasing in price at the time. Tezos performed better than others. XTZ value was estimated to have risen 1,000% during the year. It climbed from 22nd position up to 10th in the cryptocurrency ranking by Coinmarketcap.
