What is Ethereum Classic (ETC)?

Ethereum Classic (ETC) is a blockchain-based, open-source, and public distributed computing platform that has smart contract functionality. Smart contracts are applications that are run as exactly as programmed without the possibility of third party interference, downtime, and censorship. 

Ethereum Classic is made up of a blockchain ledger, cryptocurrency, and an ecosystem of services and applications that run with it. It offers a way to manage digital assets without the need for third parties. Intermediaries include banks and other financial institutions. It allows smart contracts to be developed, deployed, and executed. 

Ethereum Classic supports programmable money. It exists as the continuation of the initial or original non-forked Ethereum (ETH). ETC exists to preserve the principle that states that “code is law.” Ethereum Classic is more than just a cryptocurrency and blockchain. It improves digitized value. It allows users to create more complex contracts between them that work autonomously. 

History and Main Features of Ethereum Classic (ETC)

Ethereum Classic (ETC) was formed in October 2016 as a hard fork of Ethereum (ETH). Two years after Ethereum was launched, the investors and Ethereum community though of launching a Decentralized Autonomous Organization (DAO). 

DAO is a decentralized venture capital fund that focuses on decentralized crypto projects. The main objective was to create a stateless decentralized organization that has no employee or board of directors. Its key players would be independent investors. In May 2016, there was crowd token sale, and Ethereum managed to raise $150 million.

However, a flaw in the DAO code allowed attackers to drain $50 million from the fund. The theft caused a lot of upheavals in the Ethereum community. Ethereum made a decision to create a hard fork to restore investor confidence. The hard fork made the hacked transaction invalid. It created the Ethereum Classic (ETC).

Ethereum Classic

Ethereum Classic has the same features as Ethereum. It has a decentralized programmable blockchain, is trade on exchanges, and provides the same functionality of smart contracts and decentralized apps (DApps) as Ethereum. Its smart contract language is known as Solidity. Ethereum Classic stayed true to the original vision of a blockchain.
