What is ZCash (ZEC)?

ZCash (ZEC) is a cryptocurrency created by Zerocoin Electric Coin Company in September 2016. It is the first cryptocurrency coping the concept of blockchain technology with Zk-Snarks (proof construction or ‘secret key’ to prove certain information on a blockchain). This, as stated in the announcement of ZCash CEO Zooko Wilcox at the launching time, is the key feature, a part of the plan to make the ZCash (ZEC) project similar to Bitcoin. However, with no snags as BTC delivers. Thanks to the implemented Zk-Snarks, users can successfully make transactions without disclosing any info about the amount they transact and what the parties involved.

Keeping this idea in mind, the ZCash team gave birth to ZEC coin copying the Bitcoin technology and adding on extra features. They give semi-transparent options to the network and prevent trust issues with the high-security set-up.

ZCash (ZEC) is a virtual decentralized blockchain that enables its users to manage the ZEC transactions easily, anonymously, and secure. 

As the technology is similar to Bitcoin, the currency has a lot in common with BTC but far more to be discovered. ZEC also has features like open-source and a hard coins’ limit of 21 million. Though, in terms of maintaining compatibility and confidentiality level, the coins have a vast difference. ZCash enables to provide trailblazing cryptography use, i.e., a new way for technology development.

ZCash (ZEC) Features

ZCash (ZEC), like any other cryptocurrency, has a particular set of features that has to make the platform technically better. As a result, it should affect the price. Here are some core capabilities and features that play a role in ZEC growth.

Low-Fee Transactions that support instant coin transfer and a low-fee price for it (only 0.0001 ZEC) is a seducing feature. It makes ZCash interesting for a high amount of exchanges and top wallets. Atomic Wallet, for example.

Address and Transaction Privacy provided by the ZCash platform are of two types – shielded and transparent. In the first one, addresses and transactions can be left unrevealed as well as transaction amount and indeed the encrypted memo field content.

In the second one, transparent, transactions, and addresses are visible; they are published on the blockchain just in the same way as it’s open on the Bitcoin blockchain.

Encrypted memos is a content of the memo field, which is available in the shielded transactions. According to this feature, the sender is enabled to fill in the necessary information of the receiver and make it in an entirely encrypted way. This feature is prevalent if users want to exchange messages and instructions as well as establish some sort of conformity with the Bank Secrecy Act’ Travel’ rule.

ZCash provides multi-signature transactions demanding multiple signatures on them. It means fewer transactions may require two or more parties to confirm the transaction before funds will be available. They cannot be shielded – the transactions are transparent.

Payment Disclosure is a protocol, which enables the sender or the receiver, involved in a particular transaction process, open up specific information about the transaction to the third party. It may be needed as per requirements like proof, conformity, or visibility. Any person can disclose a memo and a value of the transaction, although, with no access to the other people’s addresses. This feature, protocol, is being under consideration at the moment, but most probably will be out next year.
